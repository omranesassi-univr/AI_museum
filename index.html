OUR UNRESOLVED PROBLEM
AI Cannot Yet Fully Cure Cancer
Despite remarkable breakthroughs, AI has not fully solved cancer diagnosis and treatment. The biological complexity of tumors, lack of diverse training data, and the inability to predict treatment resistance mean this remains one of medicine's greatest open challenges — and AI's most important frontiers.
HALL 1 · HISTORY

The Story of Artificial Intelligence

From a mathematician's thought experiment to systems that write poetry and detect disease — AI's journey spans 70 years of human ingenuity.

What is Artificial Intelligence?
AI is the simulation of human intelligence by machines — the ability to learn from experience, recognize patterns, understand language, and make decisions. It is not magic: it is mathematics applied at massive scale.
Why Was It Created?
Pioneers like Alan Turing asked: "Can machines think?" Researchers wanted to automate complex reasoning tasks — from playing chess to translating languages — that were too slow or error-prone for humans alone.

Timeline of Key Milestones

1950
Alan Turing proposes the "Turing Test" — can a machine fool a human into thinking it is also human?
1956
John McCarthy coins the term "Artificial Intelligence" at the Dartmouth Conference — the field is officially born.
1997
IBM's Deep Blue defeats world chess champion Garry Kasparov — machines prove they can beat humans at complex strategy.
2012
Deep learning breakthrough: neural networks dramatically improve image recognition, launching the modern AI era.
2016
Google DeepMind's AlphaGo defeats the world's best Go player — a game considered too complex for machines to master.
2022
ChatGPT launches and reaches 100 million users in 2 months — AI becomes a tool for everyone, everywhere.
2025–Now
AI is embedded in medicine, science, education, art, and law. The era of general AI capabilities has begun.

Machine Learning
Instead of being programmed with rules, ML systems find patterns in millions of examples. Like a child learning that "dogs" have 4 legs and fur — but at computer speed.
Neural Networks
Inspired by the human brain — thousands of connected "neurons" pass signals through layers, each layer extracting increasingly complex features from data.
Deep Learning
Neural networks with many hidden layers. With enough data and computing power, they can surpass human-level accuracy in tasks like reading scans or translating speech.
CONNECTED TO OUR PROBLEM
Why Can't AI Cure Cancer Yet?
Cancer is biologically heterogeneous — each tumor is unique. AI models trained on limited patient data struggle to generalize. Tumors mutate and evolve, making treatment resistance difficult to predict. Additionally, AI "black boxes" can't yet explain their decisions — which doctors need before acting on a diagnosis.
HALL 3 · APPLICATIONS

AI in the Real World

From your phone's face recognition to early cancer detection — AI already shapes nearly every industry on Earth.

🏥
Healthcare & Cancer Detection
AI analyzes MRI scans, histology images, and genetic data to detect tumors earlier than human radiologists. Google's DeepMind detected breast cancer with 11.5% fewer false positives than doctors. Yet full AI-guided cancer treatment remains unsolved due to tumor complexity and data limitations.

Climate & Weather Prediction

AI-powered drones and satellite imagery monitor crop health, predict yields, and detect disease outbreaks weeks before they devastate harvests — helping feed a growing global population more efficiently.

HALL 5 · ETHICS

Benefits & Risks of AI

Like fire or nuclear energy, AI is a transformative power — what matters is how humanity chooses to wield it.

BENEFITS
Earlier and more accurate disease detection, saving millions of lives through AI-powered diagnostics
Automation of dangerous and repetitive jobs, reducing workplace injury and human error
Accelerated scientific discovery — AI compressed decades of protein-folding research into months
Personalized education and healthcare made accessible to people everywhere, not just in wealthy cities
Climate modeling and clean energy optimization to help address the environmental crisis
RISKS & CONCERNS
AI bias: systems trained on unrepresentative data produce unfair outcomes — in hiring, criminal justice, and medicine
Mass unemployment: automation threatens jobs from truck driving to legal research and accounting
"Black box" problem: AI cannot explain its decisions — dangerous in high-stakes medical and legal contexts
Deepfakes and misinformation: AI-generated content already erodes public trust in photos, videos, and news
Privacy erosion: AI-powered surveillance enables authoritarian control and mass data collection without consent
ETHICAL CONCERN — OUR PROBLEM
The Ethics of AI in Cancer Care
If an AI misdiagnoses cancer, who is responsible — the hospital, the software company, or the doctor who relied on the output? Patients from underrepresented ethnic groups may receive worse AI diagnoses due to biased training data. Explainability is not a luxury: in medicine, "trust me" is not enough. These are not future problems — they are happening today.
HALL 6 · FUTURE VISION

What Comes Next?

The next two decades of AI may be the most transformative in human history. Here's what researchers, scientists, and futurists predict.

2026–28
AI diagnostic co-pilots become standard in hospitals
AI routinely assists radiologists and pathologists in most major hospitals. Survival rates for early-stage cancers improve by 20–30% in countries with AI-enabled screening programs. The "black box" problem begins to be solved through interpretable AI research.
2030
Personalized cancer vaccines powered by AI
AI designs mRNA cancer vaccines tailored to the unique mutation profile of each patient's tumor — within days of diagnosis. What took years in clinical trials begins happening in months. Cancer goes from a death sentence to a manageable condition for many types.
2035
Whole-genome AI monitoring predicts cancer before symptoms
Wearable biosensors and annual AI-powered blood tests detect cancer-associated DNA fragments years before a tumor forms, enabling preventive intervention. A new era of predictive medicine begins.
2040+
Artificial General Intelligence and its societal impact
If AGI — AI with human-level reasoning across all domains — arrives, the consequences for work, medicine, governance, and identity will be profound. Entire professions will transform. New global agreements on AI rights and accountability will be essential. Humanity will need to decide: who does AI serve?
